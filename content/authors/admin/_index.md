Christian L. Goueguel is an experienced spectroscopist with a decade of expertise in optics, chemometrics, and product development. He has broad experience in applied research for developing state-of-the-art laser-based analytical instruments.
With his expertise in chemometric modeling and keen attention to detail, Christian L. Goueguel is adept at providing comprehensive and effective solutions for problem-solving, guaranteeing the successful development and implementation of advanced analytical sensors and monitoring solutions across various industrial applications and environments.

My professional journey began as a Graduate Research Assistant at the National Research Council Canada, where I work on the Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS) technology for trace elements analysis in metallic alloys. I conducted experimental investigations on the analytical performance of the selective wavelength approaches in LIBS. A significant part of my PhD research was dedicated to exploring the effectiveness of combining LIBS with other techniques to improve the detection limits for trace impurities. I studied the integration of LIBS with laser-induced fluorescence (LIBS-LIF), resonance-enhanced LIBS (RELIBS), and resonant LIBS (RLIBS). These methods were pivotal in enhancing either the absolute or relative limits of detection in trace impurity analysis, especially in situations where damage to the sample surface must be minimized as much
as possible.

After earning my PhD, I advanced to roles including Postdoctoral Research Associate at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) and then ORISE Research Fellow at the National Energy Technology Laboratory (NETL), through which I expanded my expertise to include chemometric modeling and the development of a LIBS prototype for real-time, on-site environmental tracking. This work was carried out within the framework of the NETL’s R&D Program, which sought to develop monitoring technologies for assessing the environmental impacts of acidification, metals mobilization, and saline groundwater migration during storage of CO2 in
deep underground geologic formations.

After my postdoc, I worked as an R&D Scientist and later on as a Principal Scientist, where I applied my expertise with laser spectroscopy to investigate the analysis of nutrients in agricultural soil and plant tissue. I was deeply involved in the development of a benchtop analytical instrument that harnessed the power of LIBS technology and integrated advanced chemometric and machine-learning methodologies. Later, I transitioned industries, ascending to the role of Senior Optical Engineer at Atonarp Inc. In this capacity, I had the privilege of spearheading efforts to innovate within the medical device field, particularly focusing on a point-of-care (POC) device that integrates the LIBS technique.
